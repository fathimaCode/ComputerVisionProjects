Fire Detected - Precaution Points {{session['mode']}}

  1. Raise the Alarm: Alert everyone in the vicinity by activating fire alarms or shouting to notify others. Call the emergency fire services immediately.
  2. Evacuate the Area: Follow the evacuation route to exit the building safely. Do not use elevators; use stairs instead. Assist others, especially the elderly, children, or those with disabilities.
  3. Close Doors Behind You: Close doors to contain the fire and prevent it from spreading, but do not lock them.
  4. Avoid Smoke: If smoke is present, stay low to the ground as smoke rises. Crawl if necessary to avoid inhaling toxic fumes. Use a cloth (preferably wet) to cover your nose and mouth to filter out smoke.
  5. Do Not Re-enter the Building: Once safely outside, do not go back inside for any reason until the fire is declared under control by authorities.
  6. Use Fire Extinguisher (If Safe): If the fire is small and manageable, use a fire extinguisher, but only if you are trained to do so. Aim at the base of the fire.
  7. Stop, Drop, and Roll: If your clothes catch fire, stop immediately, drop to the ground, and roll to extinguish the flames.
  8. Stay Informed: Listen to instructions from emergency personnel and follow official guidelines.
  9. Provide First Aid: Assist anyone who is injured, if trained, or wait for emergency medical personnel to arrive.
  10. Avoid Open Flames or Electrical Devices: Do not use open flames (candles, lighters) or switch on/off electrical devices as they could ignite flammable materials or gas leaks.
